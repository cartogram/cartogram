Explain how you came to meet Andrea.
I assume we had already been acquainted, but my earliest memory of Andrea consists of us in an intense mud-fight during low-tide on a beach in Florida. We battled hard on the shore that day, and I'm pretty sure it ended in tears.

Describe a quality or virtue of Andrea’s that you admire.
Her life-long dedication to health and athleticism. She has persevered despite many discouraging events that I know could have pushed her to give up, or worse, go down a more negative path. I am so proud to see her take running as far as she has. I remember showing up to watch her run the track at York University and then hanging out the rest of the day downtown. I never doubted she'd get to the Olympics, but seeing her run in Rio was exhilarating and surreal.

Imagine Andrea became an inanimate object. What would it be, and why?
An aeropress. Compact, consistent and objectively one of the best ways to brew a quality cup of coffee. And, like the strong caffeinated beverage plunged from this intricate device, Andrea is full of grit and energy, fueling her relentless addiction to speed.

Describe a fond memory you recall having shared with Andrea.
When we were forced to huddle under a tiny umbrella in Trinity Bellwoods park. When we got her tattoo of the Olympic rings. Being super cold and waiting for the tiny penguins to show up. Meeting up at the Red Brick Cafe in Guelph or Green Beanery in Toronto. Everytime we've ever gone for runs together.

What advice can you offer Andrea for the future?
Do pychedelics and allow them change to your mind, if you want. Richard Feynman put it best, "You are under no obligation to remain the same person you were a year ago, a month ago, or even a day ago. You are here to create yourself, continuously."
