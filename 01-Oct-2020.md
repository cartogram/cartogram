## Thought Records

What has been going well and in what ways have you worked to get 40% better. Provide some examples.

A lot of big changes occured in this evaluation period: I moved to the Resiliency Team, changed roles from Front-end Developer to Production Engineer, and now take regular shifts as an Incidnet Manager On Call (IMOC). The combination of these changes has brought a lot of challenges and has forced me to be diligent and ritualistic with regard to learning and understanding as much about the Shopify platform as possible.

While the ramp-up for rols is by nature never-ending, I've made clear and measurable gains in both my technical understanding of the different layers of the Shopify stack as well as my ability to mitigate disasters and implement resiliency best practises at Shopify.

Below are some examples of the things I do on a regular basis to demonstrate these improvements:

- Read internal documentation such as the developer handbook and make continual efforts to revist playbooks and past service disruptions, while also paying close attention to #incident-room, even when not on-call or IMOC.
- Better my ability to uncover impact on the Shopify system, both during incidents to aid in mitigation tactics, but also in post-incident analysis and investigation. I've initiated pairing-sessions with more experienced colleagues on developing splunk queries and dashboards to help grow my observability skillset.
- I've solved issues with our legacy chat ops software, spy, inorder to involve myself in the betterment of the tools I rely on to properly IMOC. This has since become a passion of mine and think about it alot.
- I've experiemented with unconventional technologies in order to bring a fresh perspective to the team. I wrote an application that served as a proof-of-concept for rendering our Status Page off-platform in a standalone Cloudflare worker on the edge. This was my suggestion on a more resilient solution to what we have. I also wrote a suite of libraries geared towards providing a Front-end forward development experience for building chat ops software. In both of these examples I had to learn a lot about the platforms (cloudflare and slack) and come up with novel and creative solutions. These were done mostly outside the day-to-day tasks for my job.
- Everytime I am involved with a Service Disruption, I go out of my way to understand the domain that failed. For example, if there was an issue related to our kubernetes, I would take some time to build something on top of kubernetes inorder to better understand the different moving pieces. There was a recent incident with terraform, a service I had only known the name of at the time, and so I terraformed some services after and tried to get a base-level understanding. I never regret taking this time to learn and feel as though I have grown so much in my understanding of Shopify as a result.
- I have involved myself in a reading group geared towards exploring new methods of incident analysis. We meeet regularly to discuss the work of Nancy Leveson, Richard Cook and others. This has opened my mind to systems thinking and how systems fail and we hope to take more action to spread these ideas within Shopify.
- I pair on Front-end tasks regularly with colleagues inorder to impart the skills I have that are less typical on the team. These pairing-sessions help me get better at communicating technical concepts and build relationships with my colleagues.
- I attend conferences (virtually) on a regular basis and probably watch pre-reorded workshops on youtube, udemy, etc... I do this inorder to understand specific technologies, but it also keeps me informed of efforts in the community that I can then bring to my role at Shopify.

Where is there room for growth and/or development?

I need to get better at incident analysis and investigation. I have really relied on my colleagues in this regard so far. I struggle with it, but the more I am exposed to how others tackle investigations, the more confident I become in my own ability to do them thoroughly. I would think I need to get better at shipping solutions quickly and involving others on the team. I tend to go down a well with a problem, but sometimes I get stuck (usually on the ruby/rails side) and do not ask for help soon enough. I attribute this to my own lack of confidence or feeling so new in this role, which are issues I am currently seeking coaching/therapy to overcome. I also gravitate to novel/experimental solutions without taking more time to understand the current implementation and diagnose where it falls short. Moving forward, I'd like to get better at understanding the principles before trying to re-invent them.

I need more time. I am happy with my rate of growth and level at which I've achieved my goals, but there is always more to learn and I think I started with a particularly long road ahead of me. Shopify continues to grow at a crazy rate, so the target here is always moving. I do really enjoy and value this as being baked into the job and have the motivation to match my more senior colleagues.
